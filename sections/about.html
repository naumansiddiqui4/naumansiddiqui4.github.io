About

Building reliable insight from large-scale academic feedback is a deceptively hard problem—one that sits at the intersection of data, systems, and institutional trust. At Classranked, I work on turning high-volume course evaluation data into a dependable, scalable service institutions can rely on. I joined early and have grown alongside the platform, helping shape it from an initial idea into a production system now used by 50+ institutions and 200,000+ users, including R1 universities. A core focus has been reducing operational friction—making evaluations configurable in 15 minutes or less while preserving flexibility, performance, and meaningful analysis at scale.

My approach to this work is grounded in experience working with data at scale and performing analysis on complex, real-world datasets. During my Master’s in Computer Science at Old Dominion University, I conducted research in the Web Science & Digital Libraries Lab under Dr. Michael L. Nelson and Dr. Michele C. Weigle, where I worked on collecting, analyzing, and visualizing large-scale web and social media datasets. That experience shaped how I reason about data pipelines, performance, and system behavior under scale. Alongside this foundation, graduate study in Project Management informs how I plan, coordinate, and deliver systems that balance technical depth with execution.
